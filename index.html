How To Write a Story

Time Estimated: 1-2 hours
Awesome Rating: 9/10

It's essential for journalism majors to be able to concoct any type of engaging story for their audience. Whether it's for news, broadcasting, advertising, film, media, or visual communications - there is always something to tell.
Follow these simple steps to create an effective and engaging story for your audience!

  • Preparation

  • Time
  • Pencil or Pen
  • Paper
  • Quiet Space
  • Creative Juices

  1. Determine the theme or conflict that will happen in the story.

    .................

  2. Choose a setting for your script to take place.

    ................

  3. Establish the main character.

    This is the person/people/objects that you will be focusing on in your story.

  4. Write a 1-2 sentence summation of the plot.

    This gives you a good idea of where you want to take your story and the overall purpose of it.

  5. Start to outline your script.

    Brainstorm plot ideas on index cards.

    This block contains a photo and has a caption. We have used a
    to group and position the flickr photo with a paragraph to create a photo with a caption. -->

    Maker sure the ideas follow the theme you already established. Order them as necessary to keep the audience engaged.

  6. Write your first draft.

    Make sure to spreak out your work time accordingly.

  7. If you are scripting for a video, follow these "3 Rules Beginning Screenwriters Need to Know - Dr. Ken Archity"

    1. Everything has to be connected to everything else. NO PLOT HOLES!
    2. Think about DRAMATIC ORDER, a story doesn't have to be told in chronological, logical or psychological order.
    3. The audience is the main character of the story. Think about how the script and story appeals to your audience.

  8. Review your draft and finalize your script.

    Say your dialogue out loud to see if it sounds natural.

  9. Reread your entire script/plotline.

    Take notes on anything that doesn't make sense and fix it.

  10. Share it for someone else to review.

    Use their feedback to rewrite and fix aspects.

  11. Keep rewriting until you're satisfied.

    Happy writing!
